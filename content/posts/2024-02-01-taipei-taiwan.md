Taipei hit differently. The humidity, the neon, the constant hum of scooters - everything felt electric.

## Night Market Energy

Shilin Night Market became my ritual. Stinky tofu (an acquired taste), crispy fried chicken bigger than my face, and bubble tea so good I understood the global obsession.

## City Vibes

The mix of ultra-modern and traditional is everywhere. One minute you're in a sleek metro station, the next you're burning incense in a temple that's been here for centuries.

## Mountains in the City

Took the gondola up to Maokong. Tea plantations overlooking the entire city. The air was clearer. The view was insane.

## Food Philosophy

In Taiwan, food isn't just fuel - it's culture, it's social glue, it's art. Every meal felt like an event.

Already planning to explore more of the island. Next: heading south to Tainan.
